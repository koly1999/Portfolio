About Me

Since I was little I have always been fascinated by the way things work. I would take apart anything and everything and try to put it back together again, much to my parents annoyance. When I was in high school I joined the Science Olympiad team. My partner and I competed in 9 events, seven of them were build events. In these build events we were given a problem, and parameters that we had to follow. The goals were simple, solve the problem in the best way, and within the parameters set, and you get the highest score. We were so good at these build events that we won 1st place at Regionals, with all our events scoring first or second. We had designed everything from electric cars, hovercraft, gliders, and bridges, and we built them all in my basement. We then proceeded to compete at the University of Illinois in 2017, where we took home 3 more medals.


Art has always been important to me. Drawing is the best visual medium I have found to communicate ideas quickly to others. My father graduated from the Royal College of art in London with a degree in graphic design, and as a result he has always helped me learn to draw. He spent an exhaustive amount of time teaching me to draw in perspective, and it has become my favorite style of drawing. It is also very handy for making rather professional looking sketches of designs.


After I graduated from high school I then got accepted into rock valley college. Here I continued my interest in design by taking computer coding classes, Electrical engineering courses, and mechanical engineering classes. These classes allowed me to develop the skills to code this very website, and helped my design circuits for a variety of tasks. I hope to continue my passion for design at the University of Illinois, where hopefully they can teach me how to design properly. My goal is to eventually work for a big company and help them solve their design problems in a creative fashion.
